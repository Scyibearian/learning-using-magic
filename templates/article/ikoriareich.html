An in-depth look at German Ikoria, Reich der Behemoths

James Whicker

Ikoria, Lair of Behemoths is a set that brings to life the fantasy of a land teeming with monsters of all kinds. This makes for some quite interesting language learning.

            When using Magic the Gathering to learn languages, it can be beneficial to play with a friend to help each other understand and to make the experience more memorable for
        better vocabulary recollection. I bought a couple of bundle boxes of German Ikoria and did a draft with each of them with a friend. A bundle is ideal for 2 people to do a draft,
        with 3 packs each for drafting with 4 leftover comprising 3 packs for the winner of the draft and 1 pack for the loser of the draft. Whilst it doesn’t make for an authentic 
        competitive Magic experience, playing with players’ hands revealed can lead to better collaboration in working out what a card does, which is how I’ll be playing the decks
        featured in this article, a Mutation deck and a Cycling deck, two staple mechanics of Ikoria.

            Ikoria features a new mechanic called Mutate which allows creatures to merge with creatures already on the battlefield under the caster's control by paying an alternative
        casting cost. A mutated creature has the name, converted mana cost, colour identity, types, power and toughness of the creature at the top of the mutation pile and has the
        abilities of the top creature and of all those creature below it. A good way of playing with mutated creatures is to have the top creature fully visible and all creatures
        underneath spread downwards such that their names, art and types are hidden and only their text boxes, powers and toughnesses are visible (although powers and toughnesses
        are not relevant for creatures other than the top one). For Magic veterans, mutate is similar in nature to the Bestow mechanic.

            The German reminder text for Mutate states "Falls du diesen Zauberspruch für seine Mutationskosten wirkst, lege ihn auf oder unter eine Nicht-Mensch-Kreatur deiner Wahl,
        die du besitzt. Sie mutieren zur oben liegenden Kreatur, die zusätzlich alle Fähigkeiten der darunter liegenden hat." "Falls" is a word that like "wenn" translates to "if".
        However, "falls" always translates to "if", but the more commonly used "wenn" can also translate to "when", therefore using "falls" stresses the conditional.

        Cycling is simpler but can be just as interesting, the nature of randomness of drawing cards means exchanging a potentially bad card for a better card is a core part of most card games 
        Now onto the game. Jetzt spielen wir.

        
We start off in typical fashion with a roll off to determine which decks plays first. The mutation deck wins the 
roll off and is on the play, not the draw. The mutation deck is a Sultai (blue, black and green) deck and the cycling
deck is a Jeskai (white, blue and red) deck. The  articles on this website assume relatively little knowledge of Magic
the Gathering or the German language, but still aim to provide useful information for both advanced Magic players
and for advanced second language German speakers.
            Both players draw their hands and do not mulligan. The mutation player is quite lucky to have all 3 mana colours of their deck available in their opening hand, 
        the cycling deck has white and blue to start with, but no red and hopes to draw or cycle into this mana colour. Mutation plays an island for turn – Mutation
